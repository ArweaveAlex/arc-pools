<p><b>SkyBridge Capital</b> is a global investment firm based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, United States. It is run by founder <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Scaramucci" title="Anthony Scaramucci">Anthony Scaramucci</a>, Brett S. Messing, Raymond Nolte and Troy Gaveski.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>SkyBridge produces the SkyBridge Alternatives Conference, or <b>"SALT" Conference</b>, a capital introduction convention.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In addition to its annual flagship event in Las Vegas, SALT has hosted conferences in Singapore, Tokyo and Abu Dhabi.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It has offices located in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SkyBridge_Capital&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>SkyBridge Capital was founded in 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Scaramucci" title="Anthony Scaramucci">Anthony Scaramucci</a>, the co-founder of Oscar Capital Management.
</p><p>In June 2010, SkyBridge acquired Citigroup Alternative Investments Hedge Fund Management Group. Ray Nolte, Citigroup's former chief executive officer and chief investment officer, joined as co-managing partner and chief investment officer.
</p><p>In March 2015, the company opened its third office in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Gardens,_Florida" title="Palm Beach Gardens, Florida">Palm Beach Gardens, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The company was also the title sponsor of West Palm Beach's inaugural bike share program, SkyBike, before the program shut down in December 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 17, 2017, SkyBridge announced that it had signed a definitive purchase agreement with RON Transatlantic and <a href="/wiki/HNA_Group" title="HNA Group">HNA Capital U.S.</a>, with founder Scaramucci stepping down from his role as co-managing partner in order to take a role in Trump administration.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, on April 30, 2018, HNA and SkyBridge issued a joint statement the two sides had agreed not to pursue the deal, with Scaramucci returning as co-managing partner of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Foreign_Investment_in_the_United_States" title="Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States">Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States</a> had yet to approve HNA's takeover. The statement said that the committee had offered the two sides "a path to approval" if they made certain changes, but that neither side believed it was in its best interest to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2022, the venture arm of <a href="/wiki/FTX_(company)" title="FTX (company)">FTX</a> bought a 30 percent stake in SkyBridge for $45<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million, valuing SkyBridge at roughly $150M.<sup id="cite_ref-ft0913_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft0913-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> SkyBridge will use $40<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million from the proceeds to buy cryptocurrencies to strengthen its long-term balance sheet.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The deal additionally provided FTX with a three-year option to acquire 85 percent of the fund group.<sup id="cite_ref-ft0913_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft0913-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In November 2022, following the <a href="/wiki/FTX_(company)#November_2022_crisis_and_bankruptcy" title="FTX (company)">insolvency crisis at FTX</a>, Scaramucci said that he was trying to buy back the stake. Additionally, he added that SkyBridge took a loss on its exposure to FTX's FTT token.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
